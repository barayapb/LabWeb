Our Research

We work at the intersection of machine learning, molecular biology, and systems theory — developing computational tools and biological insights in parallel.

Genomics · Active
Single-cell RNA dynamics in neural development
Mapping transcriptional trajectories across developmental time using transformer-based models trained on multi-species atlases. We integrate velocity inference with spatial transcriptomics to reconstruct lineage decisions at single-cell resolution.
Collab: CNB-CSIC, Barcelona
Chen · Okafor · Linde · Park2022–present
Proteomics · Active
Allosteric networks in kinase drug resistance
We use graph neural networks to learn long-range communication pathways in kinase families. Our models predict which mutations disrupt allosteric signaling, enabling rational design of next-generation inhibitors that evade resistance.
Collab: EMBL Heidelberg
Soto · Nakamura · Ferreira2023–present
Synthetic Biology · Active
Genetic circuits for Boolean computation
We design minimal gene regulatory networks capable of performing AND, OR, and NOT logic in living cells. By combining in silico evolutionary search with DBTL cycles, we've demonstrated programmable computation in both E. coli and mammalian lines.
Collab: Imperial College London
Al-Rashid · Voss · Linde2021–present
Evolutionary Biology · Active
Fitness landscape navigation in protein evolution
Using deep mutational scanning datasets, we model the rugged fitness landscapes that constrain protein evolution. Our Bayesian sampling methods identify paths of high fitness through sequence space, informing enzyme engineering.
Collab: Weizmann Institute
Okafor · Park · Chen2023–present
